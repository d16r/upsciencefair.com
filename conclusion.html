Conclusion

The Discussion is the very last part of a lab. This summarizes all the parts of the lab performed, so that after the reader reads the conclusion, they know: (1) a nice summary of what happened during the lab and (2) a detailed explanation of the results. A Discussion has 6 parts, but it is broken down nicely below so it is not too overwhelming. At the end, this should be written in 1-2 paragraphs.

Rubric

Restate Purpose and Testable Question (2 points)

  • The purpose of the lab must restate the testable question.

Restate and Reject/Support Hypothesis (6 points)

  • The hypothesis must be written if, then, because format.
  • States whether or not the hypothesis is supported by the data from the experiment, or not.
  • Answer the question posed in the lab and refute or support the hypothesis and why.
    • "After analyzing the data, it shows that it [supports/denies] the hypothesis because..."

Summary of Experiment (4 points)

  • Brief summary of experiment: HOW you tested the hypothesis. Explain the procedure, but be concise.
    • "In order to test the hypothesis, a washer was tied to a string to create a pendulum..."
    • Explains possible reasons why the data turned out the way it did.

Discussion of Data (2 points)

  • This part of teh discussion must inform the reader about the relationships between the Independent Variable (IV) and the Dependent Variable (DV).
    • "The data shows that the relationship between [IV] and [DV] is..."

Error Analysis/Improvements (6 points)

  • Discuss the sources of error or outlier data points i.e. scientist did not have accurate equipment for measuring time.
  • Discuss how to minimize error: i.e. taking multiple trials...
  • "Possible sources of error in this lab were... These errors were minimized by...."

Importance/Relevance (2 points)

  • The relevance (cultural, content, etc.) of the project and the implication onto others is discussed.

Spelling and Grammar (1 point)

  • 0-1 use of personal pronouns. (0.5)
  • 0-3 spelling or grammar errors. (0.5)
