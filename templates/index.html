Science Project

Examples

Rubric

Criteria Excellent Could Be Improved Needs Substantial Improvement Not Present
Title Page - 3

Title page is neat and typed in the correct format.

(Title is centered in the middle of the page. Name, date, and period are in the bottom right hand corner.)

Title page is womewhat neat. Some of the format is incorrect.

Title page is not neat. The format is incorrect.
Question Purpose - 3

Experiment is based on an interesting and scientifically testable question that includes the independent and dependent variables.

Purpose is clearly stated and explained.

Experiment is based on a scientifically testable question that is missing the idependent or dependent variable.

Purpose is briefly stated and explained.

Experiment is based on a equestion that is not scientically testable and is missing both the idependent and dependent variables.
Purpose is overly brief and explained poorly or incorrectly.
Hypothesis - 5

Hypothesis and prediction are clearly stated in an "If__, then __ statement" and include an explanation, independent variable and depedent variable.

Hypothesis and prediction are briefly stated in an If__, then __ statement and explained. The independent variable dependent variable is incorrect or missing.

Hypothesis and prediction statements are overly brief (short) and explained porrly or incorrecrly. Both the independent and dependent vairables are incorrect or missing.
Review of Literature - 6

Thoroughly explains everything the reader needs to know in order to understand the experiment and completely follows the 4-paragraph outline given in class.

Explains some aspects of the experiment. Partially follows the 4 paragraph outline given in class.

Does not provide adequate (enough) explanations about aspects of the experiment. Does not follow the 4 paragraph outline given in class.
Materials - 3

A complete listing of the materials used in the laboratory/research is provided.

Most of the materials used in the laboratory/research are provided. Few are missing.

The material list is incomplete. Several materials are missing.
Procedure - 5

The experiment is replicable. (Some one else could repeat your steps without your help.)All steps are included.

How the materials were used is clearly explained in chronological (which one was used first, to which one was used last) order.

Drawings and photographs enhance or clarify the procedure.

Procedure is present, but incomplete or confusing.
How the materials were used is explained. Some steps are missing and not in chronological order.

Drawings and photographs are present but do not help enhance or clarify the procedure.

Procedure is missing.

How the materials were used is not clearly explained in chronological order. Several steps are missing.

Measurements are missing. The experiment is not replicable.
Drawings and photographs are not present.
Data and Results - 10

Data is quantitative (it contains what you measured, not what you wish you had measured.)
A clear, concise presentation of the data is presented. Drawings, charts, graphs, data tables are included, complete, and fully labeled.

Results are recorded clearly and accurately.
Data is clearly described in full sentences and includes a thorough explanation of what happed during the experiment.

Data is not quantitative or difficult to understand.

The data is presented in drawings, charts, graphs, data tables. However, some of the drawings, charts, graphs, data tables are missing data and labels.

Results are recorded accurately.
Data is generally described and partially explains what happened during the experiment.

The presentation of the data is not clear and precise. Several pieces of data are missing.

Drawings, charts, graphs, data tables are incomplete and not correctly labeled.

Results are recorded inaccurately.
Data is not described.
Conclusion - 20

Conclusion clearly refers to the question and purpose and states whether or not the data supports the hypothesis. Hypothesis is restated.

Hypothesis is clearly and fully supported by evidence (numbers) referenced from the data.

Explains possible reasons why the data turned out the way it did.
Possible errors that may have affected your results and changes to the experiment are explained.

Importance of project and implication on others is clearly and fully discussed.

Conclusion briefly refers to the question, purpose and may state if the data supports the hypothesis. Hypothesis is vaguely restated.

Hypothesis is partially supported by evidence (numbers) referenced in the data.

Briefly mentions one reason why the data turned out the way it did.

One possible error that may have affected your results and one change to the experiment are explained.

Importance of project and implication on others is briefly discussed.

Conclusion lacks reference to the question, purpose and hypothesis.
Hypothesis is not supported by evidence referenced in the data.

Does not attempt to explain why the data turned out.
No possible errors or changes are discussed.

Importance and implication of project is not discussed.
Presentation - 5

Report is easy to read.

Report is well organized and complete (contains a title page, purpose, hypothesis, review of literature/background, materials, procedure, data, conclusion).

Grammar and punctuation are used correctly, making it easy to understand what was meant. Third person is used throughout the report.

Report is fairly easy to read.

Report has some organization, and it is complete (contains a title page, purpose, hypothesis, review of literature/background, materials, procedure, data, conclusion), but some parts are difficult to locate.

Grammar and punctuation are generally used correctly. Sometimes it is difficult to be sure what was meant. Third person is sometimes used.

Report is difficult to read.

Report has little organization evident, and/or it is incomplete (missing one of the following: title page, purpose, hypothesis, review of literature/background, materials, procedure, data, conclusion).

Grammar and punctuation are frequently used incorrectly. Often it is difficult to be sure what was meant. Third person is not used.
